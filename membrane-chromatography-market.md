<p><p>Membrane chromatography is a technique that involves the separation of molecules based on their interactions with membranes. There are three main types of membrane chromatography: Ion Exchange Membrane Chromatography, which separates molecules based on their charge; Affinity Film Chromatography, which separates molecules based on specific interactions such as antigen-antibody binding; and Hydrophobic Interaction Membrane Chromatography, which separates molecules based on their hydrophobicity. Each type of membrane chromatography offers unique advantages and is used in a variety of applications within the biopharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
