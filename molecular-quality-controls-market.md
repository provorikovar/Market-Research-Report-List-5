<p><ul><li>Independent</li><li>Instrument Specific</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Molecular quality controls can be classified into two main market types: independent controls and instrument-specific controls. Independent controls are designed to work across multiple instruments and platforms, providing a universal quality assessment. On the other hand, instrument-specific controls are tailored to a particular instrument or platform, ensuring precise calibration and validation for that specific system. Both market types play a crucial role in maintaining the accuracy and reliability of molecular diagnostic tests, contributing to the overall quality control in the healthcare industry.</p></p>
