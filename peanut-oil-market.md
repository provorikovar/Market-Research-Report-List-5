<p><p>Peanut oil is available in two main market types: refined and unrefined. Refined peanut oil undergoes a process of filtering and refining to remove impurities, resulting in a neutral flavor and higher smoke point. It is commonly used for frying and in recipes where a mild flavor is desired. On the other hand, unrefined peanut oil is minimally processed and retains more of its natural flavor and nutrients, making it great for salad dressings and sautéing. The choice between refined and unrefined peanut oil depends on personal preference and cooking needs.</p></p>
