<p><p>Standalone Access Control Systems are independent units that operate on their own and do not require a central server to control access. They are typically used in small-scale applications where a limited number of users need access to a few doors. </p><p>Networked Access Control Systems, on the other hand, are connected to a central server that manages access control for multiple doors and users. These systems are ideal for large organizations or buildings where access needs to be monitored and controlled centrally.</p></p>
