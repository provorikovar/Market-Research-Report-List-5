<p><p>A linear drainage system refers to the management of surface water runoff through a channel or trench. The two main types of linear drainage systems are surface and underground. Surface systems are visible and typically installed within roadways or outdoor spaces to collect and divert water. Underground systems are installed beneath the ground and are used to channel water away from buildings or other structures. Both types of systems are essential for controlling water flow and preventing flooding in various environments.</p></p>
