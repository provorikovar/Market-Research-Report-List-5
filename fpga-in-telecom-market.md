There are three main types of FPGAs based on their programming technology: SRAM programmed FPGAs, which offer reprogrammability but higher power consumption; Antifuse programmed FPGAs, which provide faster performance but are one-time programmable; and EEPROM programmed FPGAs, which offer a balance between reprogrammability and performance.
